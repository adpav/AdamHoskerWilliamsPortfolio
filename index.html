I've worked on a number of design systems and in my current role, I am the sole owner of a design system which is used by multiple designers and teams. For the system to be a success and to enable designers and developers to work more efficiently, a core principle I like to stick to is in keeping the system as flexible as possible. The more complex the components become, the more rigid they can become - this can result in components which are too specific to be scaleable across scenarios. My knowledge of HTML and CSS means I can bridge the gap in conversations between designers and developers at the heart of the system and craft a language that can help maintain consistency and cohesiveness across all products and platforms.

About

Background, specialisms and processes

As a designer, it is my responsibility to minimize the cognitive effort required of users when they interact with a website or digital product. Intuitive navigation, clear site structure, and a user-friendly interface are critical factors in ensuring a seamless user experience. If users encounter confusion or uncertainty about how the system works, it can impede their ability to move through the product effectively. My goal as a designer is to eliminate any ambiguity or confusion and ensure that users have as few questions as possible about how to use the system.

To ensure that I create effective and impactful designs, I make use of various design principles and tools. Firstly, I adhere to and apply Gestalt design principles arrow_outward , which help me to create designs that are visually pleasing, well-organized, and easy to understand. I also incorporate Cognitive Biases & Principles arrow_outward to create designs that elicit specific emotions and actions from users. Additionally, I follow The Laws of UX arrow_outward , which provide guidelines for designing intuitive and effective user experiences.

In addition, I prioritize accessibility standards in my designs to ensure that they are usable and accessible to every user, regardless of their ability, device, or location.
